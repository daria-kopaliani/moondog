How often should you change cold plunge water?

A cadence chart by tub type and sanitizer — plus when to clean the filter.

The honest answer: it depends on two things — whether your plunge has a chiller and filter, and which sanitizer you run. A chiller-and-filter plunge on chlorine can go a month between changes. A DIY chest-freezer plunge on peroxide needs fresh water weekly. Here's the breakdown.

Water-change cadence

SetupSanitizerChange water every
Chiller + filterChlorine28 days
Chiller + filterHydrogen peroxide14 days
DIY / no chillerChlorine14 days
DIY / no chillerHydrogen peroxide7 days

Two factors drive the spread:

  • Filtration. A continuously running filter pulls out body oils, skin, and debris between sessions. A DIY chest-freezer build with no circulation relies entirely on the sanitizer residual, so the water fouls faster.
  • Sanitizer longevity. Chlorine holds a residual longer than hydrogen peroxide, which degrades comparatively quickly — especially in a plunge that isn't refrigerated continuously.
These are starting points, not hard rules.

Heavy use (multiple people, daily sessions) shortens every interval. If the water looks hazy, smells off, or won't hold a sanitizer reading, change it early regardless of the calendar.

Filter cleaning cadence

If your plunge has a filter cartridge, it needs its own schedule — independent of water changes:

TaskHow often
Rinse the cartridgeEvery 7 days
Deep clean (vinegar or cartridge cleaner)Every 30 days
Replace the cartridgeEvery 90 days

Most owners underestimate how fast a cartridge degrades. A clogged filter quietly drops your circulation and shortens how long the water stays clean — which is why a simple reminder beats relying on memory.

What keeps the filter and water-change cadence on track.

  • Cartridges wear out faster than most owners expect — keep a spare filter cartridge on hand for the ~90-day swap.
  • For the monthly deep clean, a cartridge cleaner (or a vinegar soak) restores flow a rinse can't.
  • When the water won't hold a reading, that's your real change signal — confirm it with test strips rather than the calendar.
